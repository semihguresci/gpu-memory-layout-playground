## 5. Interpretation vs Hypothesis
Hypothesis: throughput should rise with problem size and dispatch count until overhead is amortized, then plateau.

Observed:
- `dispatch_count=1` is always best for pure dispatch latency (`gpu_ms`) across sizes.
- Throughput-optimal dispatch now shifts toward higher counts (`128..1024`) for most sizes.
- Throughput rises strongly from small sizes and enters a high-throughput regime for larger sizes, with the exact optimum depending on dispatch count.
- Peak throughput:
  - contiguous_write: `7.85e10 elem/s` at `2^20`, `dispatch_count=1024`
  - noop: `8.02e10 elem/s` at `2^17`, `dispatch_count=1024`
![Peak Throughput by Variant](./results/charts/dispatch_basics_peak_throughput.png)

Source table:
- [dispatch_basics_peak_throughput.csv](./results/tables/dispatch_basics_peak_throughput.csv)

This matches the expected transition from overhead-bound to throughput plateau behavior.

## 7. Concepts Demonstrated
- Dispatch overhead amortization with increasing work per point
- Distinction between latency-optimal dispatch policy (`d=1`) and throughput-optimal policy (`d=64`)
- Importance of explicit correctness gating before comparing performance
- Value of separating dispatch timing from end-to-end timing

## 9. Multi-Device Collection Workflow
This experiment now supports collecting and analyzing multiple devices in-repo.

1. Run Experiment 01 and write/update `./results/tables/benchmark_results.json`.
2. Collect that run under `./runs/<device>/...`:
   - `python experiments/01_dispatch_basics/scripts/collect_run.py`
3. Rebuild aggregate tables/charts:
   - `python experiments/01_dispatch_basics/scripts/analyze_dispatch_basics.py`

## 10. Practical Rule Extracted
For this baseline workload on this GPU:
- choose low dispatch count when minimizing per-run latency
- choose high dispatch count (commonly `512` or `1024`) when maximizing steady-state throughput
- use the measured high-throughput region (`2^18` to `2^20` here) for stable cross-experiment comparisons
